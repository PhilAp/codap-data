Getting Started

Chris Olsen of Fathom's young daughter did a project with a solar panel. Check out the table and graphs to see what you can find out!

Examine the table and graphs:
  • Look at the column headers of the table. What information can you gather from the table?
  • Can you find out which light had the temperature of 98.4 degrees Celsius?
  • Look in the top graph and click on one of the dots. What do you see in the other graphs and table? Can you tell the amount of power that light had?
  • Hover over the highlighted dot in the top graph. Can you tell what the voltage of that light was?
  • Are there any relationships you seen in the data?
Attribute Key
  • Light = light number
  • Current(A) = the measure of the current in Amperes
  • Voltage (V) = the measure of voltage in volts
  • Temperature (degF) = the temperature in degrees Fahrenheit
  • power = the measure of power in watts
Create your own graph:
  • Create a new graph by clicking on the Graph icon.
  • Drag a column header, an attribute, to the graph y-axis. You should see an orange rectangle in the different areas of the graph that you can add attributes to.
  • Drag another attribute to the x-axis. Is there a relationship between the x-axis attribute and the y-axis attribute?
  • To remove an attribute from an axis, click on the axis label and select "Remove X" or "Remove Y."
