Introduction

This case table contains data about four of Jupiter's moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto. These moons were tracked for 92 days.

Examine the table and graph:
  • Look at the column headers of the table. What information can you get from the table?
  • Can you find out the angular distance of Europa from Jupiter?
  • What do you see in the case table? Can you tell from the graph at what hour Europa was 57.98 arc seconds away from Jupiter?
  • Look in the graph and click on one of the dots. Hover the highlighted dot. What information can be found there?
Attributes:
  • mjd: Modified Julian Days (decimal days since 17 November 1858)
  • year: year
  • mo: month
  • dy: day
  • hr: hour
  • mi: minute
  • limb: distance from the center of Jupiter to the edge of the planet in arc seconds
  • Io: angular distance of Io from Jupiter in arc seconds
  • Europa:angular distance of Europa from Jupiter in arc seconds
  • Ganymede: angular distance of Ganymede from Jupiter in arc seconds
  • Callisto: angular distance of Callisto from Jupiter in arc seconds
Create your own graph
  • Create a new graph by clicking on the Graph icon. What do you think those dots represent?
  • Drag a column header, an attribute, to the graph's y-axis. You should see an orange rectangle in the different areas of the graph that you can add attributes to.
  • Drag another attribute to the x-axis. Is there a relationship between the y-axis attribute and the x-axis attribute?
  • To remove an atrribute from an axis, click on the axis label and select "Remove x" or"Remove Y"
