Introduction

This case table gives the sunspot number for a random sample of 2000 days since 1951. You can find a definition of the sunspot number (which is not the number of sunspots!) at http://www.spaceweather.com/.

To break down the equation used, R = k(10g+s), where k is a scaling factor, g is the number of sunspot groups, and s is the total number of sunspots in all of the groups. A scaling factor is a factor that multiplies, or scales, a quantity.

  • Drag a column header, an attribute, to the graph y-axis. You should see an orange rectangle in the different areas of the graph that you can add attributes to.
  • Drag another attribute to the x-axis. Is there a relationship between the y-axis attribute and the x-axis attribute?
  • To remove an attribute from your graph's axis, click on the desired axis label and select "Remove X" or "Remove Y."
Attributes:
  • year = year
  • mo = month
  • day = day
  • decyear = decimal year (used for time)
  • R = sunspot number

The data are from the Sunspot Index Data Center (SIDC) in Belgium. (http://sidc.oma.be/index.php3)
