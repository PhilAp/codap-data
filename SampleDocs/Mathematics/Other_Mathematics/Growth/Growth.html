Introduction

Start with a random number between 0 and 1. Take that number and multiply it by 1 minus itself times a certain fixed number called a seed.

So, you have: x[n] = x[n-1](1 - x[n-1])·seed

Repeat this process 30 times and record the last value.

Chaos has the characteristic that small changes in the input produce disproportionately large changes in the output. (The butterfly's wingbeat alters the course of a hurricane.)

For what range of values for seed is the behavior of growth chaotic?

What part does the number of iterations (30) play in determining the chaos?
