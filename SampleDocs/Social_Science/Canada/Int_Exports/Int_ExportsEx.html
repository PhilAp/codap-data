Check out the table and graph:
  • Look at the column headers of the table. What information can you find in the table?
  • Can you find out what kinds of products Canada exports? Do they export similar products as where you live? (You may have to look up information about this)
  • Click on one of the dots in the graph. What happens in the table? What other information can you learn about the dot from the table?
  • Hover over the highlighted dot. What information is displayed?
  • Which types of exports are on the increase for different time periods?
  • Think about it:   Can you see the impact of specific trade deals that Canada has made with other countries, such as NAFTA or the Canada-USA Trade Agreement? Is there any impact from cross-border trade issues (e.g. soft wood lumber) or world events (9/11)?
Create your own graph:
  • Create a new graph by clicking on the Graph icon.
  • Drag a column header, an attribute, to the graph y-axis. You should see an orange rectangle in the different areas of the graph that you can add attributes to.
  • Drag another attribute to the x-axis. Is there a relationship between the x-axis attribute and the y-axis attribute?
  • To remove an attribute from an axis, click on the axis label and select "Remove X" or "Remove Y."
  • To color the graph according to an attribute, drag the desired attribute to the middle of the graph into the orange rectangle. Try different attributes. Is there a trend?
