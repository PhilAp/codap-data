Introduction

  • These are data of about 236 schoolchildren. Each child is represented by a dot in one of the graphs.
  • Click on a dot. Notice that the child's data is highlighted in the table on the left.
  • Try coloring the points by "age." Drag the "age" attribute into the center of the graph to create a legend. You will see that the older the child, the darker the dot. Click on one of the darker icons to see information about that child in the table.
  • To see how tall each child is, select the Height attribute. Drag it to the horizontal (x) axis. This distribution is interesting. The data cluster in two places, around 61. Click on the "sex" attribute, and put it in the center of the graph.
  • We can separate the males and females by plotting "sex" on the vertical axis of the graph.
  • Click on the ruler icon, and select "mean" to see the location of the mean. A "movable value" is helpful to read off precise values.
  • You can adjust the size of the points to make them very large or very small.
  • This should be enough to get you started. Don't be afraid to mess around and see what happens. To return to a previous plot, you can use the "undo" command in the menu.

This data comes from Lewis and Taylor (1967).
