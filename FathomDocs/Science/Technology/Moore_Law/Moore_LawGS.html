Getting Started

In 1965, Gordan Moore (co-founder of the Intel Corporation) decided to predict the evolution of the size of transistors. He hypothesized that the number of transistors in a chip will just about double every 24 months. As the technology world expanded, he was proved correct.

This case table contains the number of transistors on major central processing units (CPUs) released by Intel Corporation.

Attribute Key:
  • CPU = CPU name
  • Year = calendar year of release
  • Transistors = number of transistors
  • Since1970 = number of years since 1970

Sources:   http://www.intel.com/research/silicon/mooreslaw.htm and http://www.intel.com/labs/eml/eml_demo/EML_demo.htm (as available July 21, 2004)
