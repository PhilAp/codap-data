Introduction

In a ring of integers mod n, 0 through n-1 are the only integers there are, and ordinary addition and multiplication is done modulo the integer n. So, for example, in the ring of integers mod 5, 4+3 = 2 because you take the usual result, 7, and get the remainder after dividing by 5. Similarly, in the ring of integers mod 5, 2 * 3 is 1.
